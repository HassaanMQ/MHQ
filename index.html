The Gap

Most technical failures
aren't technical.

They're communication failures dressed in engineering language.

Engineering teams build remarkable things. But somewhere between the pull request and the executive deck, something gets lost. The nuance disappears. The context collapses. The brilliant work gets misunderstood, misrepresented, or simply ignored.

The result? Good systems get decommissioned. Great engineers get passed over. Organizations spend millions solving problems they already solved — because no one built the bridge.

"The single biggest problem in communication is the illusion that it has taken place."

— George Bernard Shaw

The MHQ Framework

Three principles.
One standard.

Each principle reinforces the others. Together they form a complete approach to technical communication that scales from code reviews to board presentations.

Message

Precision over simplicity.

Every technical concept has the right words. Finding them isn't about dumbing down — it's about leveling up. Clarity is a craft, not a compromise.

Humanity

Engineering serves people.

Full stop. The best architecture means nothing if it ignores the humans who build, maintain, and depend on it. Keep people at the centre of every technical decision.

Quality

Excellence is a standard.

Not a goal. Quality isn't achieved once — it's sustained through consistent, intentional practice at every layer. From the commit message to the strategic brief.
